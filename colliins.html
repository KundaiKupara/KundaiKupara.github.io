Collins Flat Beach: A Hidden Harbour Retreat

Tucked between North Head and Little Manly Cove in the Sydney Harbour National Park, Collins Flat Beach is a secluded gem that offers a peaceful escape from the city’s buzz. Just a short walk from Manly Wharf, this quiet stretch of sand is perfect for those seeking calm waters, scenic views, and a touch of natural magic.

What Makes it Special

- Tranquil Swimming Spot: The beach’s sheltered location makes it ideal for gentle swims and safe paddling, especially for families.
- Harbour Views: Enjoy stunning vistas across Sydney Harbour, including glimpses of The Heads and passing ferries.
- Natural Waterfall: Collins Flat is the only harbour beach in Sydney with its own waterfall, which flows gently into the sand—especially beautiful after rain.
- Picnic-Perfect: With its quiet atmosphere and shaded spots, it’s a romantic location for a picnic or a peaceful afternoon with a book.
- Wildlife Encounters: Lucky visitors might spot native birds or even a small group of penguins that occasionally frequent the area.

Getting there

Accessible only by foot, Collins Flat Beach is about a 15-minute walk from Manly Wharf, making it a rewarding destination for those willing to explore off the beaten path.
There are no facilities, so visitors should bring water, sunscreen, and snacks.
