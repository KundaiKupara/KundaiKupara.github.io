Kinokuniya Sydney – A Booklover’s Wonderland

Located in the Galleries at Town Hall, Kinokuniya Sydney is more than just a bookstore—it's a sprawling cultural hub that celebrates literature, art, and global creativity. With over 300,000 titles in stock, it’s one of the largest and most diverse bookstores in Australia.

What Makes it Special

- Multilingual Selection: Books in English, Japanese, Chinese, French, and German, catering to Sydney’s multicultural readers.
- Specialty Sections: Extensive collections of manga, graphic novels, art and design books, cookbooks, and children’s literature.
- Curated Imports: Chinese publications sourced from Taiwan and mainland China, plus a Japanese magazine subscription service.

Beyond the Books

- In-House Art Gallery: Rotating exhibitions every two weeks showcase emerging artists, giving the space a gallery-like feel.
- Café Culture: Home to Black Star Pastry, famous for its Strawberry Watermelon Cake, paired with Little Marionette coffee—a perfect reading companion.

Events & Community

Regular author talks, book launches, and cultural events—from Japanese pickling demos to YA fantasy panels—make Kinokuniya a vibrant meeting place for creatives and readers alike.
