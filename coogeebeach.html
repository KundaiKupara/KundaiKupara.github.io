Coogee Beach: Where Coastal Calm Meets Urban Charm

Nestled along Sydney’s stunning eastern coastline, Coogee Beach is a beloved seaside escape known for its gentle surf, golden sands, and relaxed atmosphere. Whether you're soaking up the sun, exploring its iconic ocean pools, or strolling the scenic coastal walk to Bondi, Coogee offers a perfect blend of natural beauty and laid-back charm. It's a place where locals unwind and visitors fall in love with the rhythm of beachside life.

Highlights

- Calm Surf & Safe Swimming: Sheltered by Wedding Cake Island, Coogee offers gentle waves ideal for families and casual swimmers.
- Ocean Pools- Explore four stunning pools:
- Wylie's Baths – historic and scenic with timber decking
- McIver's Baths – women-and-children-only, dating back to 1886
- Ross Jones Memorial Pool – turreted and photogenic
- Giles Baths – natural rock pool at the northern end
- Coastal Walk: The famous Coogee to Bondi walk starts here, offering breathtaking cliffside views and access to other beaches.
- Dining & Culture: From classic fish and chips to upscale restaurants and boutique shops, Coogee blends laid-back beach life with urban flair.

Facilities & Accessibility

- Public toilets, showers, change rooms
- Lifeguard patrols year-round
- Picnic areas, playgrounds, and BBQs
- Easily accessible via public transport (buses from the city and Bondi Junction)

Fun Fact

The name “Coogee” comes from the Aboriginal word koojah, meaning “smelly place,” referring to the seaweed that once washed ashore—though today, the beach is pristine thanks to daily cleaning.
