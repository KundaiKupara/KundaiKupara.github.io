Royal Botanic Garden Sydney: Nature’s Masterpiece by the Harbour

Located on the edge of Sydney Harbour, right beside the iconic Sydney Opera House, the Royal Botanic Garden Sydney is a 30-hectare oasis that blends natural beauty, scientific heritage, and cultural storytelling. Established in 1816, it’s Australia’s oldest scientific institution and one of the most significant botanical gardens in the world.

Highlights of the Garden

- Themed Gardens: Wander through the Palace Rose Garden, Succulent Garden, and Rainforest Walk, each showcasing diverse plant life from Australia and around the globe.
- Cadi Jam Ora – First Encounters: A powerful interpretive garden that honours the Gadigal people, the traditional custodians of the land, and explores their deep connection to Country.
- Stunning Views: With sweeping vistas of the harbour, the Opera House, and the city skyline, it’s a photographer’s dream and a peaceful retreat from the urban buzz.
- Free Entry & Daily Access: Open every day from 7 AM to sunset, with seasonal closing times ranging from 5 PM to 8 PM

Experiences & Amenities

- Guided Tours: Including Aboriginal Heritage Tours, plant science walks, and bespoke garden experiences.
- Dining & Shopping: Enjoy a meal at one of the garden cafés or browse the gift shop for botanical-themed treasures.
- Events & Exhibitions: From outdoor concerts to seasonal flower shows, the garden hosts a vibrant calendar of public events.
- Venue Hire: Popular for weddings, corporate events, and private functions in both indoor and outdoor settings.

Location

The Gardens are located at Mrs Macquaries Road, Sydney NSW 2000 and easily accessible from Circular Quay, Martin Place, and St James Station.
